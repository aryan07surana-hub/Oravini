You should design your retention analyzer as a system that breaks down any piece of content (script, reel idea, ad copy, carousel, etc.) into a timeline of attention, and then predicts exactly where and why a viewer will stay or drop off. The core idea is to simulate human attention using psychology, pacing, and proven viral patterns. Start by having the user input either a script, hook, or full content idea. Your system should immediately split that into logical phases — Hook (0–3s), Build (3–15s), Value Delivery (15–30s), Payoff (end) — and analyze each stage independently as well as together.
Within this, your first subsection should be a Second-by-Second Retention Curve Prediction, where your AI estimates audience attention over time (like a graph from 0% to 100%). You’re basically answering: “At what exact moment do people start getting bored?” Then layer in a Drop-Off Detection Engine, which flags precise moments where retention will fall (e.g., “viewer confusion at line 2,” “no pattern interrupt at 5 seconds,” “delayed payoff”). This is where you become 10x better than generic tools — don’t just say it’s bad, say exactly where it breaks.
Next, build a Hook Retention Impact Analyzer, because if the first 3 seconds fail, nothing else matters. This should evaluate how strong the opening is in terms of curiosity gap, emotional punch, clarity, and speed. Tie this into a Scroll-Stopping Score, predicting whether someone even pauses long enough to start watching. From there, move into a Pacing & Pattern Interrupt Analyzer, where your AI checks if there are enough shifts in tone, visuals, or information density to keep the brain engaged — because flat content kills retention fast.
You should also include an Information Density & Clarity Scanner — this checks whether the content is too slow (boring) or too complex (confusing). Pair this with a Cognitive Load Analyzer, which predicts if the viewer has to “think too much” at any point — a massive reason people drop off. Then add an Emotion Curve Tracker, mapping emotional highs and lows across the content. Viral content isn’t flat — it spikes curiosity, tension, surprise, or relatability. If your analyzer sees no emotional variation, it should flag it as low retention potential.
Another powerful subsection is the Payoff Strength Evaluator — does the content actually deliver on the promise of the hook? A weak or delayed payoff destroys retention and trust. Alongside this, include a Loop & Rewatch Potential Score, predicting whether the content encourages replays (a huge factor in virality). Then build a Narrative Flow Analyzer, checking if the content feels like a smooth story or a disjointed mess.
To make this insanely valuable, add a Platform-Specific Retention Model — because what works on Instagram Reels is different from YouTube Shorts or TikTok. Your analyzer should adapt its scoring based on platform behavior patterns. Then include a Audience Fit Predictor, analyzing whether the content actually matches the target audience’s attention span, interests, and expectations.
